Linguistic Elitism Should Give You the Ick

In their semi-annual words announcement, Dictionary.com added 1,228 revised definitions, 173 definitions and 173 entirely new entries. This update included Gen Z slang, with terms like “girl dinner” and “bed rotting” added to the popular online dictionary. However, Dictionary.com’s choice to add modern language introduces the discussion of whether or not these words deserve an official place in a site of record.

TikTok Loses the Right to UMG Music

Following a battle between TikTok and Universal Music Group (UMG), the biggest music company in the world has decided to revoke its licenses to their songs on the popular social media app. TikTok is a platform centered around fan-to-artist connection; the first exposure to any given song or artist often happens on the app. This move by UMG allowed the company to put a stop to the increasingly TikTok-centered music industry, effectively drawing a line in the sand. However, if other labels do not follow suit or TikTok does not renegotiate a contract with UMG, the only thing the music conglomerate has done is harm their artists and alienate fans.

When Harry Swiped Right on Sally

In the modern age of dating, apps like Tinder, Bumble and Hinge claim to consolidate your options and help you find love. However, when viewing more profiles or limiting your dating preferences are locked behind monthly subscriptions, the true motives behind these apps are called into question. Are these apps really trying to help you find your match, or is their goal to make you pay for love?
